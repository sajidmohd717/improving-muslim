Permissions & Copyright

We are open about where this content comes from and why we share it.

This platform exists in an area that many will rightly question. We have thought carefully about this, and we want to be as transparent as possible about our approach, what permissions we hold, and where we acknowledge uncertainty.

Who we are

A non-profit, open-source charity project

01

No money is made from this content

Improving Muslim does not earn advertising revenue, subscription fees, or any form of payment from the videos hosted here. There are no ads attached to the content we serve. The platform is funded voluntarily through donations from Muslims who want to see it exist.

02

Built as sadaqah for the ummah

This project is a charitable contribution to the Muslim community. The goal is to make beneficial Islamic lectures more accessible and watchable, without the distracting recommendations and harmful advertisements that come with standard video platforms. The code is open source and free for anyone to inspect or build upon.

03

Why we serve videos directly rather than embedding YouTube

Videos are served through a private CDN rather than as embedded YouTube players. This is specifically to remove ads, algorithm-driven recommendations, and distracting content that appear inside the YouTube player itself. We do not claim ownership of any content. Original links, speaker pages, and full attribution are provided throughout the platform.

How we assess content

Our labels reflect the evidence we have

A beneficial purpose, non-commercial use, attribution, or the existence of fan-run channels does not by itself grant permission to reproduce a complete lecture. We therefore distinguish between confirmed permission and content whose reuse remains unconfirmed. These labels describe our current evidence and may change as we receive clearer guidance from speakers, publishers, or other rights holders.

01

Permission confirmed

Written permission or a clear public statement allows the relevant educational or da'wah use.

02

Openly licensed

The content has an explicit reusable licence, such as an applicable Creative Commons licence, or is otherwise clearly available for reuse.

03

Permission requested

We have contacted the relevant speaker, publisher, or rights holder and are awaiting a response.

04

Permission unconfirmed

No explicit reuse licence or permission has been identified. We provide attribution and original-source links where available, remain open to guidance, and will promptly review or remove content when contacted by a rights holder.

Omar Suleiman & Yaqeen Institute

Permitted

Explicitly permitted for da'wah and educational use

Yaqeen Institute's public Watch FAQ states that their video library is "free to watch, free to share, and free to use for teaching, da'wah, and personal study." It explicitly states that teachers, imams, MSAs, and study circles may use the videos "without permission or licensing fees." Our use falls clearly within this published permission.

Ali Hammuda

Permitted

Permitted — we link back to his channel as requested

Ali Hammuda's YouTube channel About page states: "We request that anyone who reproduces material from this channel to add a link to this channel in their description." We comply with this on every series and speaker page by linking directly to his YouTube channel. No further permission was required under his own stated terms.

Asif Meherali

Madina Arabic course material is shared for public study

Yasir Qadhi

Permission Unconfirmed

Moderate operational risk — no explicit reuse licence identified

Yasir Qadhi's lectures are widely shared across official publishers and fan-run educational accounts, and beneficial non-commercial sharing appears to be commonly tolerated. However, we have not identified a blanket licence or explicit permission allowing us to self-host complete lectures. Widespread availability, attribution, and our charitable purpose do not by themselves establish permission. We include this material for non-commercial educational access, preserve the speaker's context, identify the speaker and original publisher, and link to original sources where available. We are seeking clearer guidance and will promptly review, adjust, or remove the content if Dr. Yasir Qadhi, a publisher, or another relevant rights holder contacts us.

Mufti Menk

Permitted

Permitted via Muslim Central's free distribution network

Mufti Menk is one of the most widely distributed Islamic speakers in the world. His primary distribution network is Muslim Central, a large non-profit publisher whose stated mission is to produce "free to use platforms and digital content." The free-to-use nature of this distribution network, combined with the non-commercial, educational character of our platform, places our use within the intended scope of this content.

Majed Mahmoud

Conditional

Fair use basis — polished productions require extra care

Unlike raw lecture streams, Ustadh Majed Mahmoud's official channel produces highly polished, multi-episode docuseries such as The Unseen Series and The Story of Musa. His team carefully protects their production value, and video descriptions frequently note that materials are covered by standard Section 107 Fair Use guidelines for educational summaries. We treat our use of this content with stricter caution than raw lecture material, and will adjust or remove any content promptly upon request from him or his team.

Abu Bakr Zoud

Permitted

Permitted via permissive community distribution inference

Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud does not have an explicit textual copyright waiver on his channel. However, his lectures are heavily recorded at public masajid — such as Ballarat Masjid and Masjid Al-Humera — and shared freely across communal distribution networks including Digital Mimbar. The consistent pattern of open, free community sharing across these networks provides a reasonable basis for permissive inference. We will promptly remove or adjust content upon any request from him or those representing him.

Yahya Al-Raaby

High Network Risk

Most caution required — major institutional network ownership

The greatest caution is required with Ustadh Yahya Al-Raaby. A significant portion of his most widely shared lectures are produced, filmed, and owned by major institutional media networks — most notably Eman Channel under their Light Upon Light series, and Al Furqan Islamic Centre. Corporate networks such as Eman Channel enforce strict, automated Content ID systems. We exercise maximum caution with this content, prioritise only material that appears to originate outside these institutional networks, and will immediately remove any content upon request.
