## Instagram and Meta verification

A registered business is not required for every kind of Meta Verified subscription:

- Meta Verified for creators is intended for eligible personal and creator accounts and may use government-issued identification.
- A project account named **Improving Muslim** may not be suitable for creator verification if the branded profile name cannot meet Meta's identity and naming requirements.
- Meta Verified for businesses applies to eligible professional business assets managed through a Meta Business Portfolio. Meta may request the legal business name, address, phone number, website, tax identifier, owner details, official documents, bank verification, government identification, or domain verification.
- Registration does not guarantee a badge. Meta also considers account completeness, ownership, activity, role tenure, recent account changes, security, location, and product availability.
- Meta's current business terms state that Meta Verified is unavailable to certain categories, including non-profit organisations. Forming a charity specifically to obtain the badge may therefore work against the verification goal.

Before incorporating for verification, inspect the offer shown inside the Instagram account and record whether it is the creator or business subscription path and exactly which documents it requests.

## Singapore structure options

### Sole proprietorship

This is the simplest and cheapest structure. ACRA's current government fees are S$15 for the name application plus S$100 for a one-year registration. The owner and business are not legally separate, and the owner has unlimited liability.

This is not the preferred structure for Improving Muslim because the platform handles personal data, enters content and collaboration relationships, and has material copyright exposure.

### Private company limited by shares

A private limited company is a separate legal entity and generally provides limited liability to its shareholders. It also provides a clearer identity for contracts, banking, platform accounts, content permissions, partnerships, and Meta business verification.

Current ACRA government setup fees are S$15 for the name application plus S$300 for incorporation. A company also brings recurring accounting, tax, register, company-secretary, and annual-return obligations.

**Provisional recommendation if the project is intended to become a durable organisation:**

- Legal entity: **Improving Muslim Pte. Ltd.**
- Public brand: **Improving Muslim**
- Domain: **improvingmuslim.com**
- Instagram: **@improving_muslim**

Possible fallback legal names if the direct name is unavailable:

1. Improving Muslim Learning Pte. Ltd.
2. Improving Muslim Digital Pte. Ltd.
3. Improving Muslim Media Pte. Ltd.

Keeping the legal name close to the public brand should make ownership and business-verification evidence easier to understand. Do not use terms such as "Charity," "Foundation," "Institute," or "Singapore" without checking the additional naming, referral, and representation requirements.

### Company limited by guarantee and charity registration

A genuine Singapore charity normally first needs an appropriate legal structure, such as a company limited by guarantee, society, or charitable trust, and then charity status from the Commissioner of Charities. Charity status is not created merely by registering a company.

The project's advancement-of-religion and education mission may be capable of being charitable, but a charity application requires exclusively charitable purposes and suitable governance. Current criteria include at least three governing board members, of whom at least two must be Singapore citizens or permanent residents.

This could be reconsidered when the project has a stable board, formal content permissions, financial controls, a fundraising plan, and a long-term commitment to charitable governance. It is not the recommended path when the immediate objective is Meta business verification because Meta's current terms exclude certain non-profits.

## Before choosing a structure

Resolve these questions before spending money or filing:

1. Is Sajid a Singapore citizen, permanent resident, or FIN/pass holder?
2. If a pass holder, has MOM or ICA confirmed that the pass permits registering and operating the entity?
3. Is the primary goal merely a verification badge, or will the entity receive funds, sign agreements, hire contributors, license content, or operate long term?
4. Should the organisation remain free but company-operated, or is formal charity status a genuine long-term objective?
5. Who will provide the Singapore registered-office address?
6. Is Sajid comfortable with the public-record implications of the chosen address and officer roles?
7. Who will act as company secretary and handle annual filings?
8. Which content licences and permissions must be obtained or rewritten in the entity's name?
9. How will contributions, sponsorships, advertising, and expenses be described and accounted for?

Foreigners and FIN holders should not assume that living in Singapore is enough. ACRA instructs FIN holders to confirm eligibility with their pass issuer, such as MOM or ICA.

## Name and trademark checks

Before reserving the company name:

1. Search ACRA Bizfile for identical and confusingly similar entity names.
2. Search the IPOS trademark database for **Improving Muslim** and similar education, media, software, and online-platform marks.
3. Search relevant domains and social platforms.
4. Decide whether the word mark, logo, or both require protection.

An ACRA business-name registration and a domain registration do **not** provide trademark protection. IPOS currently charges S$280 per class when an application uses only pre-approved goods and services descriptions, or S$410 per class otherwise. Get trademark advice before selecting classes if the project is likely to expand internationally.

Official references:

Because the project promotes and teaches religious material, its activity selection or application may be referred for additional review. Describe the platform and its activities accurately rather than choosing a misleading generic classification to avoid review.

## Provisional Pte. Ltd. incorporation checklist

If a private limited company is selected:

1. Confirm residency/pass eligibility.
2. Check the ACRA entity name and IPOS trademarks.
3. Confirm the proposed primary and secondary SSIC business activities using the current Bizfile/SSIC search.
4. Reserve the selected entity name through Bizfile.
5. Prepare:
   - Singapore registered-office address and accessible office hours;
   - company email address;
   - financial year end;
   - director and shareholder details;
   - controller and nominee information where applicable;
   - issued share and paid-up capital details; and
   - the model constitution or a reviewed custom constitution.
6. Incorporate through Bizfile using Singpass or an authorised corporate service provider.
7. Save the notice of incorporation, UEN, Business Profile, constitution, registers, and filing receipts in a private company-records location.
8. Appoint a locally resident company secretary within six months. A sole director cannot also be the company secretary.
9. Open a company bank account and keep project and personal money separate.
10. Establish bookkeeping, expense approval, receipts, and year-end accounts from the first transaction.
11. Register Corppass access where required for government services.
12. Record annual ACRA and IRAS filing deadlines.

Official references:

- [ACRA comparison of structures](https://www.acra.gov.sg/register/business/choosing-business-structure/)
- [ACRA eligibility and residency rules](https://www.acra.gov.sg/register/business/requirements-eligibility/)
- [ACRA company-registration guide](https://www.acra.gov.sg/register/business/registering-different-business-structures/local-company/registering-via-bizfile/)
- [ACRA directors and company secretary requirements](https://www.acra.gov.sg/register/business/registering-different-business-structures/local-company/appointing-company-directors-other-key-officers/)
- [ACRA annual-return guide](https://www.acra.gov.sg/manage/companies/legal-requirements-common-offences/filing-annual-returns-companies/steps-to-file/)
- [IRAS corporate income-tax guide](https://www.iras.gov.sg/taxes/corporate-income-tax/basics-of-corporate-income-tax/basic-guide-to-corporate-income-tax-for-companies/)

Companies currently pay corporate income tax at 17% of chargeable income, subject to applicable exemptions and rebates. A company must generally file its corporate tax return even when it has no income or makes a loss, unless IRAS grants a filing waiver. Confirm the tax treatment of voluntary support, sponsorship, advertising, grants, and benefits supplied to contributors with an accountant.

## Operational changes after incorporation

Do not treat incorporation as complete until ownership and public disclosures match the new entity.

### Website identity

- Add the full legal name and UEN to appropriate legal pages and business communications.
- State clearly who operates the website.
- Keep the public brand as **Improving Muslim**.
- Do not call the company a registered charity or IPC unless it has actually received that status.
- If a Pte. Ltd. operates the site, replace the current "non-profit, open-source charity project" wording with an accurate statement such as: "A free, open-source community learning project operated by Improving Muslim Pte. Ltd. The platform currently earns no advertising or subscription revenue."

### Privacy and personal data

- Name the operating entity in the privacy policy.
- Designate at least one Data Protection Officer. Sajid may perform this role.
- Publish the DPO's business contact information.
- Maintain a data inventory covering local storage, Firebase Authentication, Firestore, Cloudflare/R2, FormSubmit, Google Fonts, error reports, search events, popularity events, and any future analytics or payment provider.
- Document processes for access, correction, deletion, retention, breach response, consent withdrawal, and overseas data transfers.
- Review contracts and data-processing terms with service providers.

Official references:

- [PDPC Data Protection Officer requirements](https://www.pdpc.gov.sg/overview-of-pdpa/data-protection/business-owner/data-protection-officers)
- [PDPC data-protection obligations](https://www.pdpc.gov.sg/overview-of-pdpa/the-legislation/personal-data-protection-act/data-protection-obligations)

### Content rights

- Incorporation does not turn educational, attributed, freely accessible, or widely redistributed lectures into licensed content.
- Attribution, non-commercial use, beneficial intent, "fair use," or a permissive distribution inference should not be treated as substitutes for a licence without legal advice.
- Ask the actual rights holder, which may be a production company or publisher rather than the speaker.
- Store consent screenshots and full written permissions privately, not in the public GitHub repository.
- Where possible, obtain permissions that name the operating company, permitted content, hosting method, territories, duration, attribution, editing, captions/transcripts, promotional use, and termination/removal process.
- Have a Singapore intellectual-property lawyer review the copyright page and highest-risk hosted content before the company promotes the catalogue heavily or accepts funding.

### Contracts and ownership

- Record who owns the source code, brand, logo, domain, marketing assets, databases, captions, and original editorial material.
- Transfer or license the relevant existing assets to the company in writing.
- Move or formally authorise control of the domain, Cloudflare, Firebase, GitHub, email, Meta Business Portfolio, and payment accounts.
- Use written creator, contractor, volunteer, partnership, sponsorship, and content-licence terms appropriate to each relationship.

### Donations, contributions, and sponsorships

- A normal Pte. Ltd. should not imply that support payments are tax-deductible charitable donations.
- Explain who receives the money, how it will be used, whether the giver receives a benefit, whether fees are deducted, and how refunds are handled.
- Keep complete records of funds received and project expenses.
- Obtain advice before launching online fundraising. Singapore regulates charitable, benevolent, and philanthropic fundraising conducted online or offline, including appeals by non-charities.
- If sponsorship provides advertising, promotion, or another direct benefit, obtain accounting advice on income and GST treatment.

Official references:

- [Commissioner of Charities overview of fundraisers](https://www.charities.gov.sg/Pages/Fund-Raising/About-Fund-Raisers.aspx)
- [IRAS taxable and non-taxable company income](https://www.iras.gov.sg/taxes/corporate-income-tax/income-deductions-for-companies/taxable-non-taxable-income)
- [IRAS charities and non-profit GST guidance](https://www.iras.gov.sg/taxes/goods-services-tax-%28gst%29/specific-business-sectors/charities-and-non-profit-organisations)

## Charity path for later reconsideration

If the project eventually chooses formal charity status:

1. Obtain Singapore charity counsel or an experienced charity corporate service provider.
2. Confirm that the purposes are exclusively charitable and substantially benefit the community in Singapore, or determine whether a waiver is required.
3. Form a suitable legal structure, commonly a company limited by guarantee.
4. Recruit an independent and suitable governing board that meets residency requirements.
5. Adopt a governing instrument covering charitable objects, conflicts, financial controls, board succession, use of funds, and dissolution.
6. Apply to the Commissioner of Charities.
7. Do not imply IPC status. IPC status is separate and is generally required before qualifying tax-deduction receipts can be issued.
8. Reassess Meta verification separately because current Meta business terms exclude certain non-profits.
